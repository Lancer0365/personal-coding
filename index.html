Abraham Lincoln

  • Abraham Lincoln was born February 12, 1809, near Hodgenville, Kentucky.
  • Married Mary Todd November 4, 1842.
  • Fathered 4 children: Robert Todd, Edward Baker, "Willie", and Thomas "Tad".
  • First campaigned for Illinois General Assembly in 1832 but lost the seat.
  • Served as postmaster for New Salem.
  • Lincoln was admitted to the Illinois bar in 1836.
  • Lincoln won two terms in the Illinois House of Repesentatives as a whig party member.
  • In 1856, Lincoln joined the Republican party.
  • Lincoln won the Republican party nomination for Senate and delivered his House Divided speech as part of his acceptance speech in 1858.
  • Lincoln was elected the 16th president of the United States on November 4, 1860.
  • Lincoln delivered the Gettysburg Address in 1863.
  • Lincoln was re-elected and gave his second inaugral address on March 4, 1865.
  • Lincoln was assinated by John Wilkes Booth on April 14, 1865; five days after General Lee surrendered to General Grant ending the Civil War.
  • Lincoln died one day later on April 15, 1865.
